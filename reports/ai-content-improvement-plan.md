## ðŸ’¡ Best Practices for Human-Voice Content

### 1. Start with Real Conversations
**Before writing:**
- Record customer calls and note their language
- Ask sales team: "What questions do prospects actually ask?"
- Review support tickets for common pain points
- Interview executives who use the product

**Use their words:**
"Our CFO said 'I can't tell what's real anymore' when she got a deepfake email. That's the problem we're solving."

---

### 2. Add Personality & Opinion
**AI-generated content is neutral. Humans have perspectives.**

**Example:**
```
âŒ AI: "Deepfake attacks are increasingly sophisticated and difficult to detect."

âœ… Human: "Let's be honest: your employees can't spot deepfakes anymore. Neither
can mine. These attacks are too good. Trying to train people to detect them is
like training them to spot UFOs - pointless. You need technology that does the
detection for them."
```

---

### 3. Use Specific, Unusual Details
**AI generates generic patterns. Humans remember weird specifics.**

**Example:**
```
âŒ AI: "Advanced liveness detection technology"

âœ… Human: "We measure 47 biometric markers including your iris micro-movements
(which happen every 0.3 seconds when you blink) and subdermal blood flow
patterns that current deepfake AI can't replicate. It's like fingerprint-level
unique, but for your living face."
```

---

### 4. Embrace Imperfection
**AI content is polished and perfect. Real humans are messy.**

**Techniques:**
- Start sentences with "Look," or "Here's the thing:"
- Use parenthetical asides (like this one)
- Include rhetorical questions
- Admit limitations: "We can't stop every attack, but we stop 99.8%"
- Use contractions (don't, can't, won't)

---

### 5. Tell Stories with Consequences
**AI describes features. Humans explain what happens next.**

**Example:**
```
âŒ AI: "Sealfie provides complete audit trails for compliance."

wire transfer?', you pull up the Sealfie record: selfie timestamp, biometric

15 seconds. Auditor checks the box. You pass compliance. Last year, 3 companies
failed SOC 2 audits because they couldn't prove transaction approvals. Don't be
company #4."
```
